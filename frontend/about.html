AI-Powered Medical Screening Platform

What is OnchoScan?

OnchoScan is an AI-assisted cancer screening platform built for clinicians, researchers, and medical students. It uses deep learning Convolutional Neural Networks to analyse brain MRI scans and skin lesion images, then pairs those outputs with Llama 3 generated AI clinical recommendations, explainable Grad-CAM heatmaps, and downloadable clinical PDF reports — all within seconds of uploading an image.

What is Grad-CAM?

Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping (Grad-CAM) is a technique that produces a visual explanation for decisions made by CNNs. It uses the gradients flowing into the final convolutional layer to produce a coarse localisation map highlighting the important regions in the image for predicting the class.

In plain terms: it shows you exactly which part of the scan the AI looked at to reach its conclusion — making the model's reasoning transparent and clinically reviewable.

How to Read the Heatmap

🔴
Red / Hot RegionsHighest activation — the model weighted these areas most heavily when making its prediction. Pay close attention to these zones during clinical review.
🟡
Yellow / Warm RegionsModerate activation — these areas contributed meaningfully to the prediction but were less decisive than red regions.
🟢
Blue / Cool RegionsLow or no activation — these parts of the image had minimal influence on the model's decision.
✅
Clinical UseIf the heatmap highlights anatomically plausible regions (e.g. a tumor mass), it increases confidence in the prediction. Unexpected activations warrant caution.
⚠️ Risk Level System

OnchoScan assigns each prediction a risk level based on the model's confidence score and the predicted class. This is designed to help prioritise cases for clinical review.

Risk LevelConfidence RangeTypical TriggersRecommended Action
High Risk ≥ 70% on a malignant/tumour class Glioma detected, Malignant skin lesion, Meningioma with high confidence Immediate clinical review. Escalate to specialist. Do not rely on AI alone.
Medium Risk 40–70% on a malignant/tumour class Pituitary tumor, moderate confidence malignant, ambiguous lesion Schedule follow-up imaging. Monitor closely. Consider second opinion.
Low Risk High confidence on benign/no-tumor class No Tumor detected, Benign lesion with high confidence Routine monitoring. Continue standard screening schedule.

ReportLab clinical report generated
1
Image Upload & ValidationThe user uploads a medical image. The backend validates file type and size, then opens it with Pillow and converts to RGB to ensure consistent colour channel handling regardless of the original format.
2
Preprocessing PipelineThe image is resized to 224×224 pixels, converted to a PyTorch tensor, and normalised using ImageNet mean [0.485, 0.456, 0.406] and std [0.229, 0.224, 0.225]. This matches the statistics used during ResNet-18 pretraining.
3
Model InferenceThe preprocessed tensor is passed through the ResNet-18 model in evaluation mode. The output logits are converted to probabilities via softmax. The class with the highest probability is the prediction, alongside a confidence score.
4
Grad-CAM Heatmap GenerationGradients of the predicted class score with respect to the last convolutional layer's feature maps are computed. These are globally average-pooled to get channel weights, which are then used to produce a weighted activation map. The map is upsampled and overlaid on the original image.
5
Risk ScoringThe confidence score and predicted class are mapped to a 0–100 risk score. Based on this score, a risk level (High / Medium / Low) is assigned to help prioritise clinical review.
6
Llama 3 Clinical RecommendationsThe prediction, confidence, cancer type, and risk level are sent to a Llama 3 recommendation layer, which generates short, point-wise AI clinical recommendations tailored to the model output.
7
PDF Report GenerationReportLab assembles a structured clinical PDF containing the prediction result, risk gauge, class probabilities table, Grad-CAM images, patient notes, diagnostic text, and Llama 3 generated clinical recommendations. The report is saved server-side and a download link is provided.
